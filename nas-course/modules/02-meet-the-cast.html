Why this matters for AI coding

Understanding which package owns which responsibility lets you tell AI exactly where to put new code — and where NOT to put it.

🎬 The Five Actors

Each department on the lot has a star player. Here are the five you will keep running into.

📋
Catalog

The parts catalog — knows every type of neural network building block and all its possible configurations. Like a LEGO set’s parts list.

⏱️
LUT Pipeline

The measurement lab — exports blocks, ships them to the Jetson, times them, and records the results. The source of truth for “how fast is this block?”

🧠
Supernet

The shape-shifter — a single mega-network that can morph into any of thousands of smaller networks on demand, each with pre-trained weights.

🔍
Search

The strategist — translates architectures into LUT keys, computes costs, and (planned) runs the Bayesian Optimization loop.

♻️
Net2Net & Expand

The recyclers — (planned) reuse weights from similar architectures and inject new block types into the supernet.

🔧 Two Toolboxes, Two Rooms

This project runs in two separate virtual environments. Each one has exactly the tools its pipeline needs.

⏱️

.venv — LUT Pipeline

CPU-only PyTorch, Fabric (SSH), ONNX. For exporting blocks and orchestrating Jetson measurements from your laptop.

🧠

.venv-nas — NAS Pipeline

GPU PyTorch, OFA, Torchvision. For supernet operations, subnet sampling, and fine-tuning on your GPU workstation.

🏗️
Why isolation matters

In software, keeping separate environments for separate jobs is called isolation. It’s like having a woodworking shop and an electronics lab in different rooms — sawdust and solder don’t mix.

💡
The vibe-coder takeaway

When steering AI, saying “put the latency logic in search/cost.py, not in the supernet” is 10× more effective than “add some speed stuff somewhere.”
